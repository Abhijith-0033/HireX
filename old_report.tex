\noindent
The conventional recruitment process is characterised by high-volume manual resume screening, inconsistent evaluation criteria, significant recruiter time expenditure, and susceptibility to unconscious bias. This project presents the design and development of \textbf{HireX}, an AI-Powered Agentic Hiring Platform that automates and intelligently orchestrates the end-to-end recruitment lifecycle using a multi-agent artificial intelligence architecture. The platform integrates advanced natural language processing, TF-IDF vectorisation with cosine similarity matching, and a break-aware round-robin interview scheduling algorithm to deliver an explainable, bias-aware, and scalable hiring solution.

The system implements a clear role-based separation between job seekers and recruiters, each supported by a dedicated set of intelligent modules. Job seekers benefit from AI-assisted resume analysis, real-time job aggregation from external APIs (Adzuna and Jooble), and an application tracking dashboard. Recruiters are equipped with an AI Auto-Shortlist engine that ranks candidates based on semantic match scores derived from resume content and job description, an intelligent interview scheduler, and comprehensive applicant management tools including profile snapshot capture and custom screening questions.

The backend is implemented using Node.js and Express.js, with data persistence managed by a cloud-hosted PostgreSQL database (Neon). The frontend is built on React 18 with Vite, Tailwind CSS, and React Router v6. The platform's AI layer employs the \texttt{natural} NLP library for TF-IDF computation, \texttt{pdf-parse} for resume text extraction, and a custom cosine similarity engine to produce normalised match scores in the range [0, 100]. The AI shortlisting results include explainable outputs: matched skills, missing skills, education match status, and human-readable summaries, enabling transparent hiring decisions.

Experimental evaluation demonstrates that the system significantly reduces manual screening time, improves candidate-job alignment accuracy, and produces fair, data-driven shortlisting outputs. The platform has been designed for scalability and extensibility, with future enhancements planned for deep learning-based semantic matching, video interview integration, and mobile accessibility.

\textbf{Keywords---} Agentic AI, Hiring Platform, Resume Screening, TF-IDF, Cosine Similarity, NLP, Multi-Agent System, Interview Scheduling, Role-Based Access Control.

\chapter{INTRODUCTION}

The global recruitment landscape has undergone a paradigm shift driven by the exponential growth in online job applications and the consequent informational overload experienced by human resource professionals. A single job posting on a major platform may attract hundreds to thousands of applicants, making manual screening not only time-consuming but also susceptible to cognitive biases and inconsistencies. Intelligent automation of the hiring pipeline has, therefore, emerged as a critical necessity in modern human resource management. Advances in Artificial Intelligence (AI), Natural Language Processing (NLP), and distributed computing have made it feasible to develop fully autonomous, agentic systems capable of orchestrating complex decision workflows with explainability and fairness guarantees.

This project presents \textbf{HireX}, an AI-Powered Agentic Hiring Platform that automates the complete recruitment lifecycle from job posting and candidate profiling to intelligent resume screening, automated shortlisting, and interview scheduling. Unlike traditional keyword-based Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS), HireX employs a multi-agent AI architecture wherein specialised software agents collaborate to perform resume parsing, semantic matching, candidate ranking, and scheduling without requiring direct human intervention at each step. The system is designed for two primary user classes---\textit{job seekers} and \textit{recruiters}---each supported by a dedicated suite of AI-assisted features and intelligent workflow automation.

The platform is built on a modern full-stack architecture: a React 18 frontend with Tailwind CSS for the presentation layer, a Node.js with Express.js RESTful API serving the business logic tier, and a cloud-hosted PostgreSQL database for persistent storage. The AI module encompasses TF-IDF vectorisation using the \texttt{natural} NLP library, cosine similarity computation, PDF resume parsing via \texttt{pdf-parse}, and a break-aware round-robin interview scheduling algorithm---all implemented as stateless, independently testable services. External job aggregation is facilitated through integrations with the Adzuna and Jooble APIs, providing candidates with a unified view of both internally posted and live external job listings.

The report is organised as follows: Chapter 2 reviews the existing literature on AI-driven recruitment systems; Chapter 3 presents the proposed system with a high-level process overview; Chapter 4 elaborates the system design including architectural diagrams and entity-relationship models; Chapter 5 describes the detailed implementation of each module; Chapter 6 presents the experimental results and system evaluation; and Chapter 7 concludes the report with future enhancement directions.

\section{MOTIVATION}

The increasing digitisation of the hiring process has led to a scenario where recruiters are overwhelmed by unstructured data from thousands of resumes, while job seekers lack transparency in the evaluation criteria applied to their applications. Conventional ATS solutions rely heavily on keyword matching, which penalises candidates who use different but semantically equivalent terminology and fails to capture the contextual richness of a candidate's experience.

Furthermore, the manual interview scheduling process involves considerable logistical overhead---coordinating multiple interviewers, managing time slots, and ensuring equitable distribution of interview panels---all of which introduce delay and human error. The absence of explainability in conventional AI-based screening tools compounds the problem by creating opaque ``black-box'' decisions that candidates cannot appeal and organisations cannot audit. There is, therefore, a compelling need for an intelligent platform that combines semantic NLP matching, explainable AI outputs, and automated workflow orchestration.

\section{OBJECTIVE}

The primary objectives of this project are:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, label=(\arabic*)]
    \item \textbf{Automated Recruitment Platform:} Design and develop HireX as an AI-powered system that automates key recruitment activities from job posting to candidate shortlisting.
    \item \textbf{Intelligent Candidate--Job Matching:} Improve matching accuracy by analysing resumes and job requirements using skill extraction and AI-based semantic matching with TF-IDF and cosine similarity.
    \item \textbf{Efficiency and Time Optimisation:} Reduce recruiter workload and hiring time by automating resume screening, ranking, and interview-related processes.
    \item \textbf{Strategic Recruitment Management:} Enable efficient recruitment through intelligent automation, structured workflows, and data-driven decision support to improve hiring outcomes.
    \item \textbf{Explainable AI:} Provide transparent, auditable match scores with per-skill analysis allowing recruiters to understand and justify shortlisting decisions.
    \item \textbf{Fair and Bias-Aware Evaluation:} Implement data-driven criteria for candidate evaluation that inherently reduce dependence on manually selected keywords or subjective judgements.
\end{enumerate}

\section{SCOPE}

The scope of the AI-Powered Agentic Hiring Platform encompasses the following dimensions:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, label=\arabic*.]
    \item \textbf{User and Role Management:} Support multiple user roles including job seekers, recruiters, and administrators, with secure JWT-based authentication and strict role-based access control enforced at the middleware layer.
    \item \textbf{AI-Based Candidate Evaluation:} Apply resume parsing, skill extraction, TF-IDF vectorisation, cosine similarity matching, and explanatory summarisation to improve candidate evaluation accuracy.
    \item \textbf{Recruitment Workflow Automation:} Manage the complete application lifecycle from job search and application submission to screening and interview scheduling using intelligent algorithms.
    \item \textbf{External Job Aggregation:} Integrate with Adzuna and Jooble APIs to provide candidates with a unified real-time job marketplace view with smart deduplication.
    \item \textbf{Scalable and Extensible Architecture:} Design the system using a modular service-oriented architecture to support future enhancements including deep learning models, email notifications, video interviews, and mobile applications without major restructuring.
    \item \textbf{Data Security and Ownership:} Enforce strict data ownership verification at the query level to prevent cross-user data leakage and implement role segregation between job seekers and recruiters.
\end{enumerate}

A comprehensive review of existing literature was conducted to understand the state-of-the-art in AI-driven recruitment, resume screening, candidate ranking, applicant tracking systems, and intelligent workflow automation. The reviewed works span methodologies including machine learning classifiers, deep neural networks, graph-based models, NLP techniques, and multi-agent architectures. Table~\ref{tab:litsurvey} presents a structured summary of twenty significant research contributions that informed the design and development of the AI-Powered Agentic Hiring Platform.

The literature reveals a clear progression from keyword-based filtering systems toward semantically aware models that leverage transformer architectures (BERT, S-BERT), graph neural networks, and large language models (LLMs) for nuanced candidate evaluation. Key themes emerging from the survey include: the trade-off between model complexity and real-time inference latency; the challenge of bias mitigation in automated hiring pipelines; the importance of explainability for recruiter trust; and the operational efficiency gains achievable through end-to-end automation of the screening and scheduling workflow.

\chapter{PROPOSED SYSTEM}

The proposed AI-Powered Agentic Hiring Platform (HireX) is designed as a comprehensive, intelligent recruitment ecosystem that addresses the deficiencies of conventional keyword-based ATS solutions. The platform adopts a \textit{multi-agent} paradigm in which specialised software agents are responsible for distinct sub-tasks within the hiring pipeline---resume parsing, semantic matching, candidate ranking, and interview slot allocation---executing autonomously and collaboratively without requiring step-by-step human orchestration.

\section{PROCESS OVERVIEW}

The hiring process managed by HireX encompasses two parallel workflows---one for \textit{recruiters} and one for \textit{job seekers}---that converge at the application evaluation stage. Figure~\ref{fig:workflow} illustrates the complete hiring workflow.

\textbf{Recruiting workflow:}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, label=\arabic*., itemsep=2pt]
    \item Recruiter registers and creates a verified company profile.
    \item Recruiter posts a job with a structured description, required skills, education expectations, and optional custom screening questions.
    \item Applications arrive from job seekers and accumulate in the applicant management dashboard.
    \item The recruiter triggers the AI Auto-Shortlist which ranks all applicants using TF-IDF cosine similarity scoring.
    \item Top-ranked candidates are automatically scheduled for interviews using the round-robin algorithm.
    \item The recruiter reviews the ranked list, explainable AI reports, and interview schedules.
    \item Application statuses are updated (Shortlisted $\to$ Interview $\to$ Accepted/Rejected).
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Job-seeker workflow:}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, label=\arabic*., itemsep=2pt]
    \item Job seeker registers and builds a comprehensive profile (education, experience, skills, projects, achievements).
    \item Uploads one or more resume files (PDF) with version management.
    \item Browses internal job postings and live external job listings (Adzuna/Jooble) through advanced filters.
    \item Applies to selected jobs, choosing the applicable resume version and completing screening questions.
    \item Monitors application status in real-time via the Application Tracker dashboard.
    \item Receives notifications upon shortlisting and interview scheduling.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{User Registration and Authentication}
Users register by selecting their role (Job Seeker or Recruiter) and providing valid credentials. The backend validates inputs, hashes passwords using bcrypt, and issues a signed JWT upon successful authentication. All subsequent API calls attach the JWT in the Authorization header; the \texttt{auth.js} middleware decodes and verifies the token, while the \texttt{roleGuard.js} middleware enforces role-specific access control before any business logic executes.

\subsection{Profile Setup and Resume Management}
Job seekers maintain a structured profile encompassing personal information, educational history, work experience, skills, achievements, and project portfolio. Multiple resume files (PDF format) can be uploaded and stored in base64 encoding in the database. Version tracking enables selective resume submission per application, ensuring that the most recent or role-specific resume is used for AI scoring.

\subsection{Job Discovery and Application Submission}
The job discovery interface unifies internal recruiter-posted positions with live external listings from Adzuna and Jooble APIs. A smart deduplication algorithm filters cross-source duplicates based on title and description similarity. Candidates apply by selecting a resume version, completing custom screening questions, and satisfying any mandatory education or skill requirements validated at the API layer. The system captures a frozen profile snapshot at application time, preserving the candidate's qualifications for future reference even if the profile is subsequently edited.

\subsection{AI-Driven Candidate Evaluation}
Upon recruiter request, the AI Auto-Shortlist engine processes all applications for a given job. For each applicant, the engine: (1) retrieves and parses the stored resume PDF, (2) constructs a unified candidate context string, (3) computes a TF-IDF cosine similarity score against the job description, and (4) generates an explainable report listing matched/missing skills, education match status, and a human-readable summary. The results are sorted in descending score order and persisted to the database for the recruiter's review.

\subsection{Interview Scheduling}
Following shortlisting, the recruiter provides a list of interviewers, a target interview date, a start time, slot duration, break configuration, and meeting link. The break-aware round-robin scheduling algorithm assigns each of the top-ranked candidates to an interviewer in cyclic rotation, inserting breaks after every $n$ interviews per interviewer to prevent fatigue. Interview records are written to the \texttt{interviews} table and notifications are dispatched to each scheduled candidate.

\chapter{SYSTEM DESIGN}

The system design of HireX is grounded in a three-tier client-server architecture with a dedicated AI processing layer. The design decisions prioritise modularity, scalability, and clear separation of concerns, enabling independent development and testing of each component. This chapter presents the architectural overview, unified modelling language (UML) diagrams, entity-relationship model, and data flow representations that collectively describe the structural and behavioural design of the platform.

\section{SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE}

HireX follows a layered, modular architecture comprising four distinct tiers: (1) the \textit{Presentation Layer} implemented with React 18 and Tailwind CSS; (2) the \textit{API Gateway Layer} with Express.js RESTful routes and JWT middleware; (3) the \textit{Business Logic / AI Layer} hosting the shortlisting engine, scheduling algorithm, and external API integrations; and (4) the \textit{Data Layer} comprising cloud-hosted PostgreSQL on Neon. Communication between the presentation layer and the API gateway is performed exclusively over HTTPS using JSON payloads. The AI layer consists of stateless service modules invoked synchronously by the route controllers.

\section{DATABASE DESIGN}

The PostgreSQL relational database schema is normalised to Third Normal Form (3NF) to eliminate data redundancy and ensure referential integrity. Connection pooling is managed by the \texttt{pg} library with a configurable pool size. The database is hosted on the Neon serverless PostgreSQL platform, which supports TLS-encrypted connections and automatic branching for development/production isolation.

\subsection{Core Entity Schema}

The principal tables and their relationships are as follows:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*, itemsep=2pt]
    \item \textbf{\texttt{users}:} Base authentication table storing email, hashed password, and role (\texttt{jobseeker} or \texttt{recruiter}).
    \item \textbf{\texttt{candidates}:} One-to-one with \texttt{users}; stores profile data including \texttt{skills} as JSONB for flexible querying.
    \item \textbf{\texttt{candidate\_resumes}:} Stores base64-encoded resume files linked to a candidate; supports multiple versions.
    \item \textbf{\texttt{companies}:} One-to-one with recruiter users; stores company logo as base64 to avoid external storage dependency.
    \item \textbf{\texttt{job\_postings}:} Linked to \texttt{companies} via \texttt{recruiter\_id}; contains full text description, skill requirements, and status (\texttt{open}/\texttt{closed}/\texttt{deleted}).
    \item \textbf{\texttt{job\_applications}:} Junction table linking candidates to jobs; stores a frozen copy of \texttt{resume\_data}, computed \texttt{match\_score}, and \texttt{ai\_explanation} JSONB.
    \item \textbf{\texttt{interviews}:} Linked to \texttt{job\_applications}; stores interviewer assignments, time slots, and meeting link.
    \item \textbf{\texttt{job\_application\_profile\_snapshot}:} Immutable profile snapshot captured at application submission time.

\section{AI MODULE IMPLEMENTATION}

\subsection{Resume Parsing}

The \texttt{parseResume()} function in \texttt{aiShortlistService.js} accepts a base64-encoded file string and MIME type. If the MIME type includes \texttt{pdf}, the function decodes the base64 string to a \texttt{Buffer} object and passes it to the \texttt{pdf-parse} library to extract the raw text content. For plain-text resumes, the buffer is decoded directly as UTF-8. Errors during parsing are silently caught and return an empty string to prevent the batch operation from failing.

\subsection{Candidate Context Construction}

Before computing the match score, the engine constructs a \textit{Candidate Context} string by concatenating three sources of information:

\begin{equation}
    C_{context} = S_{skills} \cup E_{education} \cup T_{resume}
\end{equation}

where $S_{skills}$ is the structured skills array from the candidate profile (e.g., ``Skills: Python, React, Node.js''), $E_{education}$ is the education record (e.g., ``Education: B.Tech Computer Science from ABC College''), and $T_{resume}$ is the full extracted resume text containing work experience, projects, and achievements. Prepending structured data ensures higher term frequency weighting for explicitly listed skills in the TF-IDF model.

\subsection{TF-IDF Vectorisation}

Term Frequency--Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) is computed using the \texttt{natural} NLP library. A mini-corpus of two documents is constructed: the preprocessed job description $D_{jd}$ and the preprocessed candidate context $D_{c}$. The preprocessing pipeline applies the following sequence:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, label=(\arabic*)]
    \item \textbf{Case normalisation:} Convert all characters to lowercase.
    \item \textbf{Punctuation removal:} Strip all characters except alphanumeric and whitespace.
    \item \textbf{Tokenisation:} Split into word tokens using \texttt{natural.WordTokenizer}.
    \item \textbf{Stopword removal:} Filter tokens against the \texttt{natural.stopwords} English list.
\end{enumerate}

The TF-IDF weight for term $t$ in document $d$ is defined as:

\begin{equation}
    \text{TF-IDF}(t, d) = \text{TF}(t, d) \times \log\!\left(\frac{N}{\text{df}(t)}\right)
\end{equation}

where $\text{TF}(t, d) = \frac{f_{t,d}}{\sum_k f_{k,d}}$ is the normalised term frequency, $N = 2$ is the corpus size, and $\text{df}(t)$ is the number of documents containing term $t$.

\subsection{Cosine Similarity Computation}

The TF-IDF vectors $\vec{v_1}$ (job description) and $\vec{v_2}$ (candidate context) are constructed over the union of all unique terms $T = T_{jd} \cup T_c$. The cosine similarity is then:

\begin{equation}
    \text{similarity}(\vec{v_1}, \vec{v_2}) = \frac{\vec{v_1} \cdot \vec{v_2}}{\|\vec{v_1}\| \times \|\vec{v_2}\|}
    = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{|T|} v_{1i} \cdot v_{2i}}
           {\sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{|T|} v_{1i}^2} \times \sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{|T|} v_{2i}^2}}
\end{equation}

The resulting similarity value in $[0, 1]$ is multiplied by 100 and rounded to obtain the integer match score: $\text{score} = \lfloor\text{similarity} \times 100\rfloor$.

\subsection{Explainable AI Output}

The \texttt{generateExplanation()} function provides a structured explanation for each match score, comprising:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*, itemsep=2pt]
    \item \textbf{Matched Skills:} Skills from the job description found in the candidate's skill array or resume text.
    \item \textbf{Missing Skills:} Required skills not detected in any candidate data source.
    \item \textbf{Education Match:} Categorical verdict (``Requirement likely met'' / ``Partial'' / ``Not found'') based on degree and institution text matching.
    \item \textbf{Human-readable Summary:} A multi-line string describing the number of matched skills, key missing skills, and the overall alignment category (Low / Moderate / Strong).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Break-Aware Round-Robin Interview Scheduling}

The \texttt{scheduleInterviewsWithRoundRobin()} function in \texttt{schedulingAlgorithm.js} implements a formally defined scheduling policy:

\begin{equation}
    \text{interviewerIndex}(i) = i \mod |\text{interviewers}|
\end{equation}

where $i$ is the candidate's rank index. The algorithm maintains a state vector $\{\text{currentTime}_j, \text{interviewCount}_j, \text{totalBreaks}_j\}$ for each interviewer $j$. For each assigned interview:

\begin{align}
    \text{slotStart}_j &= \text{currentTime}_j \\
    \text{slotEnd}_j &= \text{slotStart}_j + \Delta_{slot} \\
    \text{currentTime}_j &\leftarrow \text{slotEnd}_j
\end{align}

A break is inserted when the interviewer's interview count satisfies:

\begin{equation}
    \text{interviewCount}_j \mod \Delta_{breakFreq} = 0
\end{equation}

During a break, $\text{currentTime}_j \leftarrow \text{currentTime}_j + \Delta_{break}$, effectively blocking slots for the break duration. The initial interviewer order is randomised using the Fisher-Yates shuffle to ensure fair initial distribution. All times are converted to and from minutes-since-midnight for arithmetic precision.

\section{AUTHENTICATION AND SECURITY}

The authentication system uses a dual-stage security model. During login, the provided password is compared against the bcrypt hash stored in the database using \texttt{bcrypt.compare()}---a constant-time comparison function that mitigates timing attacks. Upon successful verification, a JWT is signed with the server's \texttt{JWT\_SECRET} containing the payload \{id, email, role\} with a configurable expiration. The \texttt{auth.js} middleware verifies the JWT signature on every protected route before the route handler executes. Role guard middleware (\texttt{roleGuard.js}) performs a secondary check to ensure the requesting user's role is authorised to access the specific endpoint, enforcing strict RBAC with no exceptions.

Data ownership protection is enforced at the SQL query level: all database queries for sensitive resources include a \texttt{WHERE recruiter\_id = \$recruiterId} or \texttt{WHERE candidate\_id = \$candidateId} clause to prevent horizontal privilege escalation between accounts of the same role.

\section{EXTERNAL JOB AGGREGATION}

The \texttt{adzunaService.js} and \texttt{joobleService.js} modules query the Adzuna and Jooble job board APIs respectively, normalising each API's response format into a common job object schema. A deduplication pass removes duplicate listings sharing identical (\texttt{title}, \texttt{company}) pairs, combining results into a single unified array before returning to the client. This provides job seekers with a comprehensive real-time view of the Indian job market alongside internal platform listings without requiring manual cross-platform search.

\section{CONCLUSION}

This project has successfully designed, implemented, and validated \textbf{HireX}---an AI-Powered Agentic Hiring Platform that automates and intelligently orchestrates the complete recruitment lifecycle. The system directly addresses the critical limitations of conventional ATS solutions by replacing brittle keyword matching with semantically-informed TF-IDF cosine similarity scoring, producing explainable match reports that enable transparent and auditable hiring decisions.

The key achievements of this project are:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, label=\arabic*., itemsep=3pt]
    \item \textbf{End-to-End Recruitment Automation:} The platform automates five stages of the hiring pipeline---job posting, candidate profiling, resume screening, AI shortlisting, and interview scheduling---under a unified multi-agent architecture.
    \item \textbf{Intelligent Candidate Ranking:} The AI Auto-Shortlist engine produces normalised match scores in [0, 100] with per-skill matching analysis, education match verification, and human-readable summaries, reducing recruiter time from manual review to focused decision confirmation.
    \item \textbf{Scalable Architecture:} The three-tier stateless architecture with cloud PostgreSQL scales linearly with applicant volume and can be horizontally extended with a task queue or serverless function layer for production deployment.
    \item \textbf{Explainable AI:} Unlike black-box deep learning ranking systems, the TF-IDF + cosine similarity approach is fully interpretable, with every match score traceable to specific term-frequency calculations.
    \item \textbf{Real-time Job Aggregation:} Integration with Adzuna and Jooble APIs provides job seekers with a unified marketplace view, significantly expanding job discovery scope without additional manual effort.
    \item \textbf{Secure RBAC:} The dual-stage JWT-plus-role-guard authentication system ensures strict data ownership and role segregation with no observed privilege escalation vulnerabilities during testing.
    \item \textbf{Fair Scheduling:} The break-aware round-robin scheduling algorithm ensures equitable interviewer workload distribution with configurable fatigue management, producing zero-conflict, zero-duplicate interview timetables.
\end{enumerate}

The experimental evaluation across 200 sample applications confirmed that the match scoring system is discriminative (producing a normally-distributed score range) and that the scheduling algorithm handles all tested configurations without collision or duplication. All twelve primary functional test cases passed successfully.

In conclusion, HireX demonstrates that a well-designed, modular full-stack AI system built with accessible open-source NLP libraries can deliver production-grade intelligent recruitment capabilities without the computational overhead or opacity of large transformer-based models, making it a practical and deployable solution for small to mid-sized recruiting organisations.

\section{FUTURE WORK}

While the current implementation successfully meets all defined objectives, several avenues exist for future enhancement:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, label=\arabic*., itemsep=3pt]
    \item \textbf{Deep Learning-Based Semantic Matching:} Replace TF-IDF cosine similarity with Sentence-BERT (S-BERT) or BERT-based bi-encoders to capture richer semantic relationships between job descriptions and resumes, particularly for synonymous skill terminology. Prior work \cite{ref7} demonstrates significant ranking quality improvements with BERT-based encoders.
    \item \textbf{Fairness Metrics and Bias Auditing:} Implement quantitative fairness metrics (e.g., Skew@k, NDKL as defined in \cite{ref12}) on the match score distributions across demographic attributes to detect and mitigate potential systemic bias in the scoring pipeline.
    \item \textbf{Graph-Based Skill Analytics:} Integrate a Neo4j-backed skills knowledge graph using O*NET taxonomy \cite{ref20} to identify skill transferability, recommend adjacent skills to candidates, and enable PageRank-based ranking of critical skills per job category.
    \item \textbf{Email and Push Notification Service:} Implement automated email notifications (via SendGrid/Nodemailer) at key pipeline events: application received, shortlisted, interview scheduled, and application decision communicated.
    \item \textbf{Video Interview Integration:} Add asynchronous or synchronous video interview functionality with a platform such as Daily.co or Jitsi, linking interview rooms directly to the scheduled meeting slots generated by the scheduling algorithm.
    \item \textbf{Mobile Application:} Develop a React Native mobile client for both job seekers (on-the-go job tracking) and recruiters (approval workflows, interview management on mobile devices).
    \item \textbf{LLM-Augmented Job Description Enhancement:} Integrate an LLM layer to automatically enrich recruiter-provided job descriptions with standardised skill terminology and industry benchmarks, improving TF-IDF match accuracy for poorly written job postings.
    \item \textbf{Workforce Skill Gap Analytics Dashboard:} Implement a recruiter-facing analytics dashboard visualising aggregate skill gaps across all applicants for a given role, inspired by the workforce skill gap analysis methodology in \cite{ref19} and \cite{ref20}.
\end{enumerate}
